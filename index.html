Historical & Contextual Analysis

Timeframe & Exhibition Context:

Symbiosis.live was developed in 2022 during a period of increasing global attention to posthuman ecologies,
digital-bio entanglement, and the role of automation in defining "life." It was exhibited at the
Gwangju Art & Media Center (South Korea), engaging with an audience familiar with media arts,
bio-art, and critical technological practices.
Source: morakana.com

Authors’ Motivation:

  • Tiri Kananuruk explores voice, interface, and embodied perception through technological filters. (Mana Contemporary)
  • Sebastián Morales questions the agency of bots and networks, treating internet traffic as organic behavior. (Mana Contemporary)

Contextual Drivers:

  • Rise of bio-art and hybrid media systems in contemporary critical art. (Experimenta Biomess)
  • Public concern about AI, surveillance, and human/non-human boundaries. (SFPC Networked Performance)
  • Post-COVID reflections on interaction, networks, and remote perception. (ACM Bioart Paper)

Visual & Aesthetic Representation

  • Physical‑digital lab aesthetic: microscope, bioreactor, cables, screens — evoking scientific rigour. (morakana.com)
  • Continuation of biomess style: transparent vessels & living media in gallery context. (Experimenta)
  • Microscopy visuals: high-magnification imagery as abstract art form. (TIME on Carl Strüwe)
  • Responsive interface lighting: bots’ interactions visualized via light/signal cues. (SFPC Networked Performance)
  • Fluid materiality: transparency, bubbles, flows echo soft robotics aesthetics. (Frontiers in Robotics)
  • Situates within bioart community: echoes SymbioticA tradition & cross-media labs. (Visual Symbiosis)
